How to Use PercentMaster - Percentage Calculator Help

Welcome to the Help section! Here’s how to make the most of our percentage calculator tool.

1. Basic Usage

Enter numbers in the provided fields, and results will update automatically as you type. All calculations happen in your browser—no data is stored.

2. Available Calculators

  • Percentage of a Number: Find what percentage of a number is.
    Formula:
    \( \text{Result} = \left( \frac{X}{100} \right) \times Y \)

    Example: What is 25% of 200? Result = \( \left( \frac{25}{100} \right) \times 200 = 50 \).
  • Find the Percentage: Determine what percentage one number is of another.
    Formula:
    \( \text{Result} = \left( \frac{X}{Y} \right) \times 100 \)

    Example: 50 is what percent of 200? Result = \( \left( \frac{50}{200} \right) \times 100 = 25\% \).
  • Percentage Increase: Calculate a value increased by a percentage.
    Formula:
    \( \text{Result} = Y + \left( Y \times \frac{X}{100} \right) \)

    Example: What is 20% more than 150? Result = \( 150 + \left( 150 \times \frac{20}{100} \right) = 180 \).
  • Percentage Decrease: Calculate a value decreased by a percentage.
    Formula:
    \( \text{Result} = Y - \left( Y \times \frac{X}{100} \right) \)

    Example: What is 20% less than 150? Result = \( 150 - \left( 150 \times \frac{20}{100} \right) = 120 \).
  • Percentage Change: Find the percentage difference between two values.
    Formula:
    \( \text{Result} = \left( \frac{Y - X}{X} \right) \times 100 \)

    Example: Percentage change from 100 to 120? Result = \( \left( \frac{120 - 100}{100} \right) \times 100 = 20\% \).

3. Tips

  • Use positive or negative numbers as needed.
  • Results are rounded to 2 decimal places for clarity.
  • If you get an error, ensure all fields have valid numbers.
